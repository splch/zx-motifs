========================================================================
        COMPREHENSIVE ANALYSIS: irr_layered
        A Data-Driven Quantum Circuit from ZX Motif Phylogeny
========================================================================

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

irr_layered is a 5-qubit, 18-gate quantum circuit automatically
discovered by the Irreducible Composition strategy of the ZX Motif
Phylogeny pipeline. It was constructed by identifying three structural
motifs that survive full ZX-calculus reduction — phase_gadget_2t,
hadamard_sandwich, and cluster_chain — and composing them in a
two-layer architecture with round-robin qubit allocation.

The circuit is structurally novel (composite novelty 0.513, PCA
isolation 0.779) and exhibits clean ZX properties: 38.2% vertex
compression, 11 irreducible interior vertices, and verified tensor
preservation. Its ZX simplification trajectory and motif survival
pattern make it a useful case study for illustrating the pipeline.

However, honest evaluation shows this circuit has no identified
operational advantage. Its output state factors into single-qubit
rotations and a rotated Bell pair — producible with ~8 gates instead
of 18. The 10 extra gates are overhead from the motif composition
procedure, not useful computation. The head-to-head comparisons
against industry standards (QFT, Grover, QAOA, etc.) that appeared
in the initial comparison report are not meaningful: these circuits
solve entirely different problems, and generic metric comparisons
between unrelated algorithms do not establish operational competitiveness.

irr_layered is best understood as a pipeline demonstration specimen,
not a candidate for practical deployment.


2. CIRCUIT CONSTRUCTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Strategy: Irreducible Composition

The Irreducible Composition strategy identifies motifs that survive
the most aggressive ZX-calculus simplification (full_reduce). These
"irreducible" patterns represent structural elements that cannot be
eliminated by the spider fusion, interior complementation, pivot, or
local complementation rules.

From the full corpus of 78 algorithms, the pipeline identified 10
motifs surviving full reduction. Three with qubit footprints <= 2
were selected for layered composition:

  1. phase_gadget_2t  (2 qubits)  — CX-T-CX sandwich
  2. hadamard_sandwich (1 qubit)  — H-S-H basis rotation
  3. cluster_chain     (2 qubits) — H-CZ graph state link

2.2 Layered Architecture

The three motifs are applied sequentially with round-robin qubit
offset shifting (+1 per motif), then the entire sequence repeats for
a second layer:

  Layer 1:
    phase_gadget_2t   on q[0,1]  — offset 0
    hadamard_sandwich on q[1]    — offset 1
    cluster_chain     on q[2,3]  — offset 2

  Layer 2 (shifted):
    cluster_chain     on q[3,4]  — offset 0 (wrapped)
    phase_gadget_2t   on q[3,4]  — offset 1
    hadamard_sandwich on q[0]    — offset 2
    cluster_chain     on q[1,2]  — offset 0 (final)

Key observations:

  - The graph is STABLE from INTERIOR_CLIFFORD through FULL_REDUCE
    (21V/22E at all three levels). This means the pivot and local
    complementation rules of full_reduce find nothing further to
    simplify beyond what interior_clifford_simp already captured.
    The remaining 11 interior vertices are genuinely irreducible.

  - Spider fusion eliminates 8 vertices (34->26), merging adjacent
    same-type spiders with compatible phases.

  - Interior Clifford simplification removes 5 more (26->21),
    absorbing degree-1 Clifford spiders into their neighbours.

  - The Hadamard-edge ratio jumps from 0.286 (raw) to 0.818
    (reduced), indicating the simplified form is dominated by
    Hadamard connections — characteristic of graph-state-like
    structure.

3.2 Surviving Interior Structure (after full_reduce)

  11 interior Z-spiders:
    Phase 0   : 7 vertices (structural backbone)
    Phase pi/4 : 2 vertices (T-gate contributions — irreducible)
    Phase pi/2 : 2 vertices (Clifford phases, Hadamard-edge blocked)

  Edge composition:
    18 Hadamard edges (82%)
    4 Simple edges   (18%)

  The two T-phase (pi/4) vertices cannot be eliminated by any ZX
  rewrite rule. They represent the non-Clifford computational content
  of the circuit. The two pi/2 vertices survive because they are
  sandwiched between Hadamard edges that block absorption.

4. OUTPUT STATE ANALYSIS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 State Factorisation

The 5-qubit output state |psi> = U|00000> decomposes as a tensor
product:

  |psi> = |phi_0> (x) |phi_12> (x) |phi_3> (x) |0>_4

where:
  q0:   |phi_0> = (|0> - i|1>) / sqrt(2)       (a Y+ eigenstate)
  q1-2: |phi_12> = (|00> + i|11>) / sqrt(2)     (rotated Bell pair)
  q3:   |phi_3>  ~ 0.707|0> + (0.5+0.5i)|1>     (single-qubit rotation)
  q4:   |0>                                      (untouched)

This was confirmed by SVD rank-1 decomposition at each partition:
  - SVD of (q1q2q3, q0): singular values [1.0, 0.0]  (rank 1)
  - SVD of (q3, q1q2):   singular values [1.0, 0.0]  (rank 1)

The q1-q2 pair is in a maximally entangled state with concurrence
1.0, but it is NOT one of the four standard Bell states (fidelity
0.5 against each). It is locally equivalent to |Phi+> via a pi/2
phase rotation on q2.

4.2 What This Means

The circuit uses 18 gates and 5 qubits to produce:
  - Two single-qubit rotations (q0, q3)
  - One rotated Bell pair (q1, q2)
  - One idle qubit (q4)

A purpose-built circuit achieving the same output state needs:
  - q0: Ry + Rz = 2 gates
  - q3: Ry + Rz = 2 gates
  - q1-q2: H + CX + Rz = 3 gates
  - q4: 0 gates
  - TOTAL: 7 gates, depth ~3

The 18-gate circuit has ~2.5x the gate count necessary to produce
its own output. The overhead comes from the motif composition
procedure: each motif is applied as an opaque template without
knowledge of what the preceding motifs have done, so there is no
cross-motif cancellation. ZX-calculus could in principle simplify
this (the full_reduce form is more compact), but circuit extraction
from the reduced graph actually produces 24 gates (more than the
original), because the reduced graph's Hadamard-dominated structure
doesn't map efficiently back to a gate sequence.

4.5 Comparison to Trivial Bell Pair Generation

  Under depolarising noise at p = 0.01:
    H+CX (2 gates, 1 two-qubit):        F = 0.989

  For the specific task of "produce an entangled pair on q1-q2,"

  can be structurally unique simply because no one had reason to build
  it. The novelty scores measure how different the circuit is from
  known algorithms, not whether that difference is useful.


8. WHAT THIS ANALYSIS DEMONSTRATES ABOUT THE PIPELINE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

irr_layered is useful as a case study — not because it outperforms
known algorithms, but because it illustrates concretely how each stage
of the pipeline works and where the current approach falls short.

What works:

  (a) MOTIF SELECTION: The irreducible composition strategy correctly
      identifies ZX-surviving motifs. The three selected motifs
      (phase_gadget_2t, hadamard_sandwich, cluster_chain) do survive
      full_reduce in the composed circuit — 8 cluster_chain instances
      and 2 hadamard_sandwich instances persist through all
      simplification levels.

  (b) STRUCTURAL NOVELTY: The motif fingerprint genuinely differs
      from all corpus algorithms. The combination of entanglement
      and error-correction motifs produces a structural profile that
      occupies uncharted PCA space. The pipeline discovers circuits
      that are structurally unlike known algorithms.

  (c) VALID CIRCUITS: The output is a verified unitary (error
      2.47e-16) with preserved tensor semantics through ZX reduction.
      The pipeline does not produce garbage.

  (d) ZX ANALYSIS PIPELINE: The full simplification trajectory, motif
      survival table, and compression metrics provide genuine insight
      into the circuit's structure.

What does not work:

  (e) NO TASK AWARENESS: The composition procedure has no objective
      function. It composes motifs because they are irreducible, not
      because the resulting circuit does something useful. The output
      state — single-qubit rotations plus a rotated Bell pair —
      is an artefact of the specific motif overlap, not a designed
      capability. A pipeline that optimises for a target task (e.g.,
      entanglement fidelity, ansatz expressibility, error detection
      probability) would produce more meaningful circuits.

  (f) COMPOSITION OVERHEAD: The motif-by-motif construction creates
      ~2.5x gate overhead versus a purpose-built circuit producing
      the same output state. Cross-motif cancellations are invisible
      to the template-based builder. ZX simplification can identify
      these redundancies (the reduced graph is 38% smaller) but
      circuit extraction from the reduced graph doesn't recover them
      efficiently (24 gates vs 18 original).

  (g) MEANINGLESS COMPARISONS: Comparing irr_layered against QFT or
      Grover on generic metrics (gate count, depth, entropy) does not
      establish competitiveness. These circuits solve different
      problems. bit_flip_code needs redundant gates because redundancy
      IS error correction. Beating it on gate count is not a finding.

  (h) SCALING IS PADDING: Fixed gate count across varying qubit counts
      means additional qubits are idle. This is an artefact of the
      composition procedure, not a scaling property.

Implication for the paper:

  irr_layered demonstrates that the ZX motif phylogeny pipeline can
  (1) identify structurally irreducible building blocks, (2) compose
  them into novel valid circuits, and (3) characterise the results
  through multi-level ZX analysis. It does not demonstrate that the
  pipeline discovers operationally useful algorithms. The gap between
  structural novelty and practical utility is the central open
  problem this work surfaces.
